# High Signal Handoff

## Primary Goal
Get to $5k MRR ASAP. High Signal is the active commercial focus.

## Positioning
High Signal is the intelligence/data curator for marketing and product decisions.

Core promise:

> High Signal watches markets, communities, competitors, AI assistants, and public sources, then tells SaaS founders what changed, why it matters, and what to do next.

Do not position it as:

- Generic stock signals
- Generic "world-flow"
- A random insight feed
- A broad content generator

## Paid Wedge
Start with AI visibility + competitor intelligence for SaaS companies.

Offer:

> "I'll show where your SaaS is invisible to AI assistants/search, which competitors are recommended instead, and exactly what content/positioning to fix."

Target customers:

- B2B SaaS founders
- AI/devtool startups
- Bootstrapped SaaS companies
- Agencies that can resell audits
- Products in crowded "which tool should I use?" categories

MRR path:

- 5 customers x $1,000/mo
- 10 customers x $500/mo

## Product Modules
High Signal should absorb MentionPilot as a module.

Modules:

- **Mention Intelligence** — AI assistant visibility, competitor mentions, citation gaps
- **Community Intelligence** — Reddit/community complaints, repeated needs, workflow gaps
- **Market Intelligence** — market/company/category changes that affect buying or positioning
- **Agent Evaluation Intelligence** — whether a product is legible, credible, and recommendable to AI/buyer agents
- **Opportunity Radar** — what should we build/test/watch/avoid?

## Self-Marketing Loop
High Signal content should market itself.

Loop:

1. Detect a real visibility/category gap.
2. Publish a sharp public artifact.
3. Name categories, competitors, and gaps.
4. Make founders wonder: "Are we invisible too?"
5. CTA: "Check your AI visibility" / "Get your competitor gap report."
6. Convert audit requests into paid monitoring.

Best public content formats:

- AI Visibility Map: "Which tools show up when buyers ask ChatGPT for X?"
- Competitor teardown: "Why Competitor A gets recommended and you don't"
- Citation gap report: "What pages AI assistants cite before recommending tools"
- Winner/loser table for a SaaS category
- Public scorecards for bootstrapped SaaS / devtools
- Weekly "what SaaS buyers are asking/complaining about" brief

## Marketing Tool Relationship
Build a separate fancy marketing tool/playground, but make High Signal the data layer.

Architecture:

```text
Sources -> High Signal -> Campaign Brief -> Marketing Studio -> Assets
```

High Signal answers:

- What changed?
- Who is affected?
- Why does it matter?
- What evidence supports it?
- What should we do next?

Marketing Studio answers:

- How do we package this into assets?

Marketing Studio outputs:

- Landing page sections
- Competitor comparison pages
- LinkedIn posts
- Cold emails
- Launch copy
- Carousel outlines
- Short reel scripts
- Weekly campaign briefs
- Founder update posts

Important boundary:

- High Signal is what customers pay for.
- Marketing Studio is the activation/workbench that helps sell and fulfill High Signal.

## Portfolio Boundaries
- **High Signal** — active commercial bet
- **MentionPilot** — merge into High Signal as Mention Intelligence
- **SaaS Maker** — internal execution/fleet/task/agent control layer
- **CodeVetter** — parked playground/future option for code-quality workflows only
- **SaaS ideas list** — backlog/reference, not current focus

## Near-Term Execution
Do not build a broad dashboard first. Sell reports first.

First version:

1. Pick one niche: devtools, AI SaaS, or bootstrapped SaaS.
2. Create 3 sample AI visibility audits.
3. Publish public category artifacts.
4. DM/email founders with one specific finding.
5. Sell $500-$1,000/mo recurring monitoring.
6. Build only the repeated parts after 3-5 customers ask for the same thing.

## One-Sentence Direction
High Signal should become the evidence engine that finds what SaaS companies should say, fix, publish, and monitor so they get discovered by AI assistants and buyers.
